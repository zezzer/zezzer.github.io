ABOUT ME

Hi! I'm Zemei, a 3rd year undergrad Computer Science major and Statistics minor at UC Berkeley.



I'm currently interested in gaining knowledge for and prepping for a career in machine learning and data science.

I would describe myself as a cooperative team player and a fast learner with a good attention to detail.

I'm experimenting with Hadoop and OpenCV right now.

In addition to programming, I enjoy violin, reading, baking, and derping around.

I grew up in the Silicon Valley.

I love to think and have long held a fascination with philosophy and psychology.

I persistently strive to challenge myself, be adaptable, and improve. For example, I used to be extremely shy as a kid. I decided to challenge myself by taking a speech class in high school. It was admittedly uncomfortable at first but I soon found I was enjoying myself. I ended up joining the speech team and winning 1st in a novice tournament for DUO interp. Life is a continual journey of growing as a person.

INTERESTS

I've been playing violin for over 14 years.



After self teaching the violin for the 4 years preceding, I joined the UC Berkeley Symphony Orchestra for violin.

Back when I attended UC San Diego, I was concertmaster of Intermission Orchestra, a student led ensemble that plays video game and film music.

I enjoy playing chamber music.

I love music because I love learning and improving - when faced with a technique issue, I try to focus on getting down to the root cause of the issue (usually something to do with bow awareness and/or posture). Better technique results in a greater range of expression!

When creating music, my ultimate goal is to play expressively and connect with the audience.
